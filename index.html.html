Why This Was Never Fixed
This Problem Needs Infrastructure, Not Apps

Four compounding barriers have prevented any solution from reaching scale — each one feeds into the next.

01
Millions of Small Producers
India has 140M+ smallholder farms averaging under 1 hectare. No single entity has ever aggregated them at trade-grade volume without physical infrastructure on the ground.
140M+
Smallholder farms in India
02
Quality & Standardization
Export buyers require grade-certified, consistently sized produce with documented traceability. Achieving this across millions of micro-farms demands end-to-end QA systems — not just spot checks.
<5%
Indian farms are export-grade certified
03
Logistics + Working Capital
Coordinating cold-chain transport from remote Northeast India farms to ports requires simultaneous financing, compliance documentation, and real-time coordination — a complexity no app can solve alone.
30–90
Days typical payment delay for farmers
04
Trust Deficit on Both Sides
Farmers distrust remote buyers who don't pay on time. Buyers distrust quality claims from unknown suppliers. Rebuilding this trust requires T+0 payments, physical FPC relationships, and consistent delivery — all at once.
T+0
FarmBridge pays farmers instantly on delivery
